Ideation

"We wanted to visualize what 21x faster means."

Deall claims to make job searching 21x faster for both employers and job seekers. But what does it really mean? We broke down the job application process into three stages -- the search, the evaluation, the execution. Each step could be simplified and made easier for the user. We created function such as one-button apply and automated search to implement this fast application process.

Prototyping

Fast, simple, easy to use

We built a prototype highlighting the most used features of a job-search app: job recommedations, deadline notifications, and custom job lists. We implemented each of these functionalities with speed and ease of use in mind.

Testing

Voices, and more voices

We tested out prototype with classmates and also people on usertesting.com. Users said they liked the simplicity of the site. It allowed them to quickly and intuitively apply for jobs they liked. They also commented that they liked the clean interface and the easily nativation through different screens and functionalities. Some participants reported that the simplicity of the interface was also a weakness because the interface lacked advanced filtering and sorting features. We were also able to turn the feedbacks into design changes, such as cleaner navigation and clearer UI indicators.

Key Takeaway

The project is a design-thinking masterclass. We were priviledged to work with an existing start-up and help them come up with a UI design. Throughout the project, I learned to think iteratively and constantly critique my own design to perfect it.
